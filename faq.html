FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

About SGDA

How does SGDA work?
During the off-season when the conference is not being organized, SGDA mostly operates on our Discord server, where people can advertise opportunities such as game jams, industry events, or job offers. You can also check us out on our Twitter or Instagram for updates and shoutouts to student developers or organizations. Our social media platforms function as a public network, so feel free to @ us to share your club’s games or news!
How do I become a part of SDGA?
If you’re a student leader who hasn’t contacted us or have not been contacted, you can ask our current outreach officer about being added to our mailing list, obtaining access to our Discord server, and being added to our website as a partner. You can also ask your student organization about us.
How can I help SGDA?
Consider donating to our GoFundMe page for our 2020 conference! You can also help by being an active member of our community on social media and our Discord server by asking and answering questions, sharing resources, showing your game development work, and generally being supportive of other developers.
What does the future of SGDA look like?
As of 2019, the organization is undergoing the process of becoming a non-profit, thus making it possible for us to become a sustainable, self-governing entity that can continue to support learning game developers in more tangible ways. It’s a long process, so keep an eye out for updates in the following years!


Questions about the SGDA Summit



What is the conference and what can I do there?
The conference, previously known as the SGDA Summit, is a single day gathering for any interested student, indie, or professional developer. It hosts speaker panels from a variety of different backgrounds including indie dev, AAA dev, engineering, art, and business. Each of the host clubs are offered to organize workshops on useful game dev skills, technologies, and any other kind of knowledge they’d like to give back to the community. Host clubs are also encouraged to send in their student games to be shown in a games showcase, where attendees can play their games and celebrate their work. Whatever your profession is, there will be something of interest to you! There will also be opportunities to relax, play games, and form connections with fellow game developers across California. Oh, there will be giveaways, swag, and food too!
Do I need to have experience making games?
NOPE! All you need is a passion for games and a desire to learn about game development. The conference is designed to educate people of any skill level, and it should help set the stage for your future endeavors!
How much does the Summit cost?
The event is free to enter, parking not including! However, monetary donations are appreciated to keep the event free and smoothly run by the SGDA volunteers who are donating their time and energy to make it happen. Check out our support tab for more information.
What do I need to bring? What should I wear?
We suggest you bring an open mind and something with which to take notes. If you wish to participate in workshops, you should bring your laptop as well. Equipment requirements will be listed in each workshop blurb on the SGDA website. Semi-casual dress is recommended! Although industry professionals will be present, you should not treat the summit like a job interview. You may submit your resume when you register, but you do not need to bring it with you.
Do I need to stay for the whole duration?
Nope! However, if you know for sure you will not be able to stay for the whole duration, please consider registering for "general admission" when EventBrite tickets go up. It is only fair to those who are able to enjoy the event to its fullest.


Questions about the Summer Game Jam

Who can attend?
All students over the age of 18 are welcomed to attend! All participants must follow the GGJ Code of Conduct.
Is there a limit on registration?
Unfortunately, our venue only has enough space and we only have enough food to accommodate 200 people for UCR (Southern California site) and 100 people for UCSC (Northern California site). The earlier you register, the more likely you will be accepted, so sign up now!
What do I bring?
Please bring your student IDs in order to check in and any hardware or software you need to develop your game. Because this is an overnight event, consider bringing a pillow, sleeping bag, and anything else you need to make your stay comfortable.
Will there be food?
UCR: Food and snacks will be provided during the event.
UCSC: Only snacks will be provided during the event.
Are there travel reimbursements?
Unfortunately, we do not support any form of travel reimbursements. Rest-assured, we are working hard on the quality of the event to make sure all attendees have a meaningful and great experience!
Are teams required?
You aren’t required to form a team prior to the event. However, we do want everyone to be on a team to work on an awesome game together during the game jam! We will have a social in the beginning of the event to help you get into a team if you don’t have one already.
What if I’m not very experienced?
No worries! We will have workshops at the start of the event for beginner jammers. Also, whether you need help on programming, art, music, or design, SGDA will have experienced student and professional mentors to guide you in the right direction. The Global Game Jam is a friendly, networking environment and a great way to gain more experience not only on game development, but also working in a team!
How do I sign up?
You must fill out our google form as well as register for whichever venue you want to go to on the Global Game Jam website.
